Understanding Your Organization

Acme Corp Healthcare operates 12 facilities across the Mid-Atlantic region, serving over 340,000 patients annually. Your organization has invested heavily in clinical excellence, and your patient satisfaction scores consistently rank in the top quartile regionally.

But your digital infrastructure tells a different story. The patient portal, built six years ago on a proprietary platform, has become a bottleneck. Appointment scheduling requires phone calls during business hours. Lab results take 48 to 72 hours to appear online. The website, running on an outdated CMS with no clear content governance, generates more confusion than conversions.

Your IT team is stretched across compliance, EHR maintenance, and day-to-day operations. Every digital improvement competes with keeping the existing systems running. The technology decisions made five years ago are now technical debt your team inherits every morning.

You need a partner who is not intimidated by legacy systems, who understands healthcare compliance requirements intimately, and who can modernize your digital infrastructure without disrupting the clinical operations your patients depend on.

The Challenge

Six systemic issues that are affecting patient experience, operational efficiency, and competitive position.

01
Legacy Platform Lock-in
Your current CMS is proprietary, end-of-life, and requires specialized vendor support for every change. Simple content updates take days instead of minutes. Your team cannot move at the speed your patients expect.
02
Patient Portal Friction
Only 23% of eligible patients have activated their portal accounts. Of those, fewer than half use it monthly. The interface requires too many steps for basic tasks, and mobile responsiveness is unreliable across devices.
03
Integration Fragility
Your EHR, billing system, scheduling platform, and patient communication tools operate in silos. Data flows are held together by custom scripts that break when any vendor updates their API. One integration failure affects the entire patient journey.
04
Compliance Gaps
WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility requirements, HIPAA technical safeguards, and state-specific health data regulations each demand attention. Your current platform was not built with compliance automation, so every audit is a manual, expensive process.
05
Provider Directory Chaos
Your website lists 180+ providers across 12 locations. The information is outdated within weeks of publication. Patients search for a specialist, find incorrect availability, and call the wrong office. It creates friction before they even walk through your doors.
06
Competitive Digital Gap
Regional competitors have launched patient-first digital experiences with online scheduling, telehealth intake, and proactive health content. Every month your digital platform stays static, patients with options are choosing providers who make their lives easier.

The Opportunity

Healthcare organizations that invest in patient-centered digital experiences see measurable returns across acquisition, retention, and operational costs. The data is clear: patients choose convenience when clinical quality is comparable.

A modern, open-source platform gives your team control over content, integrations, and compliance without vendor dependency. It reduces the cost per change, accelerates time to publish, and creates a foundation that scales as you add facilities, services, and patient populations.

Most importantly, it gives your patients what they actually want: the ability to find a doctor, book an appointment, access their records, and communicate with their care team without picking up the phone.
