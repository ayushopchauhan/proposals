Understanding Your Business

Acme Corp has built a strong reputation in financial technology over the past five years. Your core payment processing platform serves over 2,000 businesses, and your team has grown to 80 people across product, engineering, and operations.

But your digital presence has not kept pace with your product. The current website was designed three years ago, before your latest product releases, before your Series B, and before your customer base tripled. It reflects where Acme Corp was, not where it is heading.

Your platform's user interface, while functional, shows signs of technical debt. Support tickets related to navigation confusion and workflow friction have increased quarter over quarter. Meanwhile, competitors in the fintech space are investing heavily in UX, making it the new battleground for customer acquisition and retention.

You need a partner who understands both the strategic and technical dimensions of digital transformation. One who can redesign your public-facing brand while simultaneously improving the product experience that your customers interact with daily.

The Challenge

Five friction points that are costing Acme Corp growth, trust, and engineering bandwidth.

01
Brand and Product Disconnect
Your website communicates reliability and trust, but your product interface tells a different story. Users experience inconsistency between your marketing promise and their daily platform experience. This gap erodes confidence during onboarding and increases churn in the first 90 days.
02
Conversion Friction
Your current website converts visitors to demo requests at roughly 1.8%. Industry benchmarks for fintech SaaS sit between 3.5% and 5.2%. Every month this gap persists, you leave qualified leads on the table for competitors who invested in conversion-optimized experiences.
03
Scaling Without a Design System
As your product has grown, features have been added without a unified design system. The result: inconsistent components, duplicated patterns, and a growing backlog of UI fixes that compete with feature development for engineering time.
04
Content That Does Not Convert
Your blog publishes regularly, but traffic to conversion is nearly flat. The content architecture lacks clear pathways from educational content to product pages. Users read and leave without taking action.
05
Accessibility and Compliance Risk
Financial services face increasing regulatory scrutiny on digital accessibility. Your current website has dozens of WCAG AA violations that expose Acme Corp to compliance risk and exclude potential customers who rely on assistive technologies.

The Opportunity

The fintech companies winning market share right now share one trait: they treat digital experience as a core product, not a marketing afterthought.

A redesigned website that accurately represents Acme Corp's capabilities could drive a 2x improvement in demo conversion rates. Based on your current traffic of approximately 12,000 monthly visitors, that translates to an estimated 180 additional qualified leads per month.

A unified product design system would reduce your UI development backlog by an estimated 40%, freeing your engineering team to focus on features that drive revenue rather than fixing inconsistencies. And a content strategy built around conversion architecture could transform your blog from a traffic source into a pipeline generator.
